ABOUT ME



I am a Ph.D. candidate (A.B.D.) in Department of Political Science of the University of Iowa.

My research interest primarily lies in language policy and political communication. I particularly focuses on how language policy affects citizens' political attitudes, relations, and value system and how the government-to-citizen communications are conducted and impact public opinions.


As a methodologist, I am interested in the applications and developments of survey experiments, text analysis, network analysis, spatial analysis, and multilevel analysis. In international relations, I explore the peaceful rising in the contemporary interdependent international system, the cultural diplomacy and diplomatic institutions, and the influences of the economic inequality on the mass political beliefs and opinions.
